About

Hi, I’m Dhanvardini.

Every product starts with a person and a need. I learned that long before I knew the word “product,” growing up in Thanjavur, a temple town in Tamil Nadu, where I led my school as pupil leader by doing one thing well: listening to what people needed, then making it happen. At SASTRA I studied computer science, but a product design course in my junior year is what clicked. I did not just want to build software, I wanted to decide what was worth building.

At Optum, part of UnitedHealth Group, I went from intern to Product Manager by treating every release as a hypothesis. I owned AI powered discovery for more than 5 million members, instrumented the funnels, ran the experiments, and shipped the changes the data asked for. The metrics mattered, and so did the 50+ engineers and PMs I mentored, because a product is only as strong as the team behind it.

I came to Boston for my master’s at Northeastern to go deeper on AI and agentic systems. Along the way I led a community of 1,500 aspiring product managers, ran hackathons for 400+ teams, and taught the craft I love. It taught me that the best product work scales people, not just features.

Today I build AI products the way I always have: start with the user, prove it with data, and bring engineering along as partners. I turn complex AI into products people trust, and I build the communities that build them.
