Wilson Sports App

An end-to-end sports-commerce app built on Wilson's brand promise, Empower Every Human to Live Like an Athlete. I designed the full journey from first open to reorder, and used AI to produce the on-brand copy and imagery at every step.

My role
Product Manager
Context
Self-directed UX & AI case study
Scope
28-screen commerce app
AI tools
ChatGPT & Claude

Overview

A whole shopping experience, designed like a brand.

Wilson sells championship-grade equipment, but a generic storefront does not carry the feeling of that brand. I designed the Wilson Sports App as a complete, athlete-first commerce experience: every screen, from the splash promise to the order-tracking timeline, is built to make an everyday shopper feel like a professional gearing up. Then I used AI as a production partner to write and generate the on-brand content that fills it.

The opportunity

Sporting-goods apps tend to be interchangeable catalogs: search, list, buy. Wilson's identity, empowering every human to live like an athlete, was not expressed anywhere in the shopping flow. The opportunity was to design a branded, athlete-first experience where the copy, imagery, urgency, and post-purchase moments all reinforce that promise, and to prove a PM can produce that content quickly with AI rather than waiting on a full studio.

Design principles

Three principles kept a large surface area coherent, so a 28-screen app still felt like one considered product.

Brand in every pixel

Wilson red, athletic imagery, and empowering language on every screen, so the shopping trip feels like gearing up for a game, not filling a cart.

One confident path

A single, obvious route from onboarding to checkout to reorder, with the most-used actions always within thumb reach.

Trust at every step

Transparent pricing, clear order tracking, real reviews, and readable terms, so a shopper never has to wonder what happens next.

The end-to-end experience

I designed the whole loop, not just a few hero screens. Each stage was built as a real, usable flow.

Flows worth calling out

Shop by sport

The home surface leads with six sport categories (basketball, tennis, baseball, football, golf, volleyball), so a shopper starts from how they actually think about their game.

Flash Deal urgency

A time-boxed deal event with a live countdown, a savings badge, and social proof (“847 athletes shopping these deals now”) turns browsing into action without feeling like a trick.

Product detail that sells

Ratings, tour-grade specs, size and hand options, stock and shipping status, and a clear price-and-save line give a serious buyer everything they need in one screen.

Transparent checkout

An itemized order summary with subtotal, shipping, and estimated tax before payment, so the total is never a surprise at the end.

Order tracking

A four-step Ordered to Delivered timeline with plain-language status updates keeps the post-purchase anxiety low.

Account & reorder

Saved orders, multiple addresses, and stored cards make the second purchase far faster than the first.

AI integrations

AI as a content production partner.

The differentiator was not just the design, it was how the design got filled. Rather than ship lorem text or wait on a studio, I used ChatGPT and Claude to generate on-brand, purpose-built content for specific screens, each with a clear product intent. I wrote the prompts as a PM writes a brief: audience, goal, constraints, and tone.

WhereWhat AI producedProduct intent

Profile & settingsA friendly, on-brand avatar illustrationReplace impersonal default icons with brand-aligned warmth
Order confirmationA warm, celebratory success messageReduce post-purchase anxiety and reinforce the choice

The PM angle

Each AI use was tied to a metric or a job: urgency to conversion, description to justified price, upsell to order value, confirmation to retention. AI was the tool; the product judgment about what content each screen needed, and why, was the work.

Craft & inclusivity

A consistent brand system

Wilson red, athletic photography, and one type and button language across all 28 screens keep a large app feeling like a single product.

Dark mode

A full light and dark theme so the app is comfortable in any lighting, matched to how people actually shop at night.

Localization

Language settings with a worked German example (Dunkler Modus, Benachrichtigungen) show the experience holds up beyond English.

What I took away

Designing a full commerce app end to end taught me that brand is a system, not a logo: it has to survive every screen, including the boring ones like terms and settings. And using AI to produce the content showed me the modern PM leverage point, I could take an experience from empty wireframe to on-brand and believable fast, as long as I brought the judgment about what each screen needed to do. The through-line I carried out: decide what the content must accomplish first, then let AI help you produce it at speed.
