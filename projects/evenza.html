Evenza

A hyperlocal event discovery and ticketing app for events within 1 to 10 miles: tonight, happening now, walkable, under $20, on campus, or free. The positioning is "Airbnb for events happening near you."

My role
Product Manager
Context
Self-directed UX case study
Scope
12 core pages, 3 USP pillars
View the wireframes

The problem

Most event apps push large, ticketed, far-away events, which leaves a gap for what is actually near you tonight. I framed Evenza around hyperlocal discovery: events within 1 to 10 miles, filtered by intent like "tonight," "happening now," "walkable," "under $20," "on campus," or "free," so spontaneous, nearby plans become as easy to find as distant headliners. The bet is that the most relevant event is rarely the biggest one; it is the one you can walk to in twenty minutes.

The positioning

POSITIONING

"Airbnb for events happening near you." Where Airbnb made local rooms discoverable and bookable, Evenza does the same for the cafe gig, the campus open mic, or the free workshop down the street, with distance and intent leading every result.

Who it's for

I designed for college students, young professionals, new immigrants, and solo explorers, including people without cars who want cheap or free walkable plans. A secondary side serves small venues, creators, student orgs, and local businesses who host these events. Both groups need a fast path from interest to attendance, often alongside friends and often on short notice.

Three USP pillars

Evenza rests on three pillars, each one reinforcing the same promise: that nearby, spontaneous plans should be the easiest kind to find and commit to.

Hyperlocal discovery

Distance leads every card, so a listing reads "450m away" before anything else, making proximity the first thing you judge.

Small-creator empowerment

Cafes, students, and instructors host events with easy posting, QR entry, group booking, and lightweight analytics, so small hosts can run an event without a ticketing platform.

Spontaneous & social plans

Going out is treated as something you decide on now, with friends, rather than weeks ahead, matching how nearby plans actually form.

The design: 12 core pages

I designed twelve core pages spanning the full journey from first open to attending, keeping a tone that is clean, warm, youthful, and community-first throughout.

Get in

Splash, onboarding, and auth set the tone and tune the experience to your interests and radius from the start.

Discover

A home of hyperlocal categories and an explore view with map and filters surface what is near you right now.

Decide and book

Event details, ticket selection and checkout, and a ticket confirmation with QR code and countdown carry you to attendance.

Manage and connect

My tickets, groups for shared planning where everyone pays individually, alerts tuned to interests, radius, and "tonight" nudges, plus profile and settings.

Designing for spontaneity

The hardest design constraint was time: a "tonight" plan only works if discovery, decision, and ticket all happen in minutes. So I let intent filters do the heavy lifting, put distance and price where the eye lands first, and kept group planning frictionless by having everyone pay individually rather than forcing one person to front the cost. Every screen is tuned to shrink the gap between "I'm bored" and "I'm going."

What I'd measure

Because the whole bet is on nearby and spontaneous, I would track the discover-to-ticket funnel for hyperlocal events: how often a "tonight" or "walkable" listing turns into a confirmed ticket, and how quickly. I would also watch creator-side health, since easy posting and QR entry only matter if small hosts keep listing. The product wins when nearby, low-cost plans feel effortless to commit to.
