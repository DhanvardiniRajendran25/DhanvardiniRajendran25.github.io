TripMate

A unified travel super-app with one promise: "Plan less. Experience more!" It folds transport, stays, dining, and destination activities into a single booking and trip-management experience, replacing the cluster of apps travelers juggle today.

The problem

Travelers today juggle separate apps for flights, hotels, transit, tickets, and events, and the cost shows up as cognitive overload, missed details like refund rules, baggage limits, and rooming, and poor coordination across a group. Working on this as a five-person group project, I framed TripMate around that fragmentation: the friction is not any single booking, it is the constant app-switching and re-entry that surrounds the whole trip.

The promise

VALUE PROPOSITION

"Plan less. Experience more!" TripMate unifies transport, stays, dining, and destination activities into one inclusive booking and trip-management experience, so the time saved on logistics goes back into the trip itself.

The pain points

I anchored the work in specific pains that travelers, especially those moving as a group, feel both at the desk while planning and on a phone mid-trip.

Multi-app dependency

Flights, hotels, transit, tickets, and events each live in a different app, with no single place that holds the trip.

Repetitive data entry

The same names, dates, and payment details get re-typed across every separate booking surface.

Hidden fees and no central itinerary

Inconsistent refund rules and surprise fees hide in the fine print, and nothing pulls the whole plan together.

Group-planning friction

Coordinating across people happens in side channels, with information overload and limited support making it worse.

Eight objectives

We translated those pains into eight product objectives, each one a direct answer to a friction point above.

Unify & simplify

Unify bookings into one place and simplify the multi-step flows that fragment the trip today.

Personalize & build trust

Enhance personalization and increase trust and transparency, surfacing fees and refund rules rather than hiding them.

Centralize & collaborate

Centralize trip management in one itinerary and enable collaboration so groups coordinate inside the app.

Notify & scale

Deliver smart notifications at the right moment and design the experience to scale across trip types.

The design

I focused the design on the two objectives that attack the core pain: centralized management and simplified flows. One itinerary holds the whole trip, transparency on fees and refunds is built into booking rather than buried, and collaboration features let a group coordinate without a side channel. Each decision aimed to cut app-switching and repetitive data entry, and to keep the next step obvious whether planning at a desk or moving through an airport.

What I'd measure

The promise is "Plan less. Experience more," so I would measure consolidation: how many trip needs get handled inside TripMate instead of a separate app, alongside repeat use across the full arc of a trip rather than only at booking. For the collaboration objective I would watch whether groups actually coordinate in-app. The super-app wins when it removes app-switching, not just adds another booking surface.
